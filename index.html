One of the influential civil rights leader

Here's a time line of King's life:

  • January 15, 1929: Martin Luther King Jr. was born in Atlanta, Georgia.
  • 1944: King entered Morehouse College at the age of 15.
  • 1951:King graduated from Morehouse College with a Bachelor of Arts degree in sociology.
  • 1953:King married Coretta Scott, whom he met while attending Boston University.
  • 1955:Rosa Parks was arrested for refusing to give up her bus seat, leading to the Montgomery Bus Boycott. King is chosen to lead the Montgomery Improvement Association and becomes a prominent figure in the civil rights movement.
  • 1960: King moved to Atlanta and becomes co-pastor, with his father, of Ebenezer Baptist Church.
  • 1963:King leads the Birmingham Campaign, which includes sit-ins, marches, and other protests against racial segregation. He writes his famous "Letter from Birmingham Jail." On August 28, he delivers his iconic "I Have a Dream" speech during the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom.
  • 1964: King was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his work in the civil rights movement.
  • 1965:King and the SCLC lead the Selma to Montgomery marches to advocate for voting rights. The marches result in the passage of the Voting Rights Act of 1965.
  • 1968: King plans the Poor People's Campaign, advocating for economic justice and an end to poverty.
  • April 4, 1968: King was assassinated in Memphis, Tennessee.
